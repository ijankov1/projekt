Overview

The second generation Toyota Celica, introduced in 1978, continued the legacy of its predecessor. It featured a refined design and enhanced performance, further solidifying Toyota's presence in the sports car market.

Key Features

  • Continuation of sporty and compact design
  • Available in coupe and liftback body styles
  • Upgraded engine options, including a 1.6, 1.8, 2.0, 2.2 and a 2.4 inline-four
  • Rear-wheel drive configuration
  • Distinctive styling cues, reflecting the era's automotive trends

Legacy

The second generation Celica built upon the success of its predecessor, further establishing Toyota's commitment to delivering stylish and high-performance vehicles. Its impact contributed to the continued evolution of the Celica lineup.
